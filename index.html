About Mental Health

According to the Centers for Diseases Control and Prevention's website, mental health is defined as it "includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make healthy choices." Mental health plays an essential role in every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence to adulthood.

Although there is no single cause for mental health, there are many factors that can contribute it. Some of these factors are:

  • Various Childhood Experiences such as tramua and a history of abuse (i.e. Child Abuse, Sexual Assault, etc.)
  • Experiences related to other ongoing (chronic) medical conditions, (i.e. cancer, diabetes, etc.)
  • Having feelings of loneliness/isolation
  • Use of alcohol and/or drugs
